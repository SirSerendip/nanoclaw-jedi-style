*End game:* JOTF's moat is not the games, tools, or collision briefs — it's the compounding backend. The business follows a razor/blade architecture informed by Sam Lessin's "goes to zero" thesis (application-layer AI gets copied in hours; only compounding assets survive).

*Razor (give away / low cost):*
• Innovation games, software, tools
• Game deliverables, templates
• Jedi persona prompts (visible)
• Signal collision briefs (public-facing, demonstrate the methodology)

*Blade (sell / protect):*
• Signal stream (live, stale tomorrow — must stay live or moat erodes)
• Neo4j InnoGraph (compounding graph — the core asset)
• Graph access at invocation time (invisible, architectural dependency)
• Collision engine (methodology + live corpus)
• Client usage patterns (flywheel)

*Three structural requirements:*
1. InnoGraph must be live, not static — signal stream going dark = graph freezes = moat erodes
2. Jedi personas must draw from the graph at invocation time, not just be prompts
3. Dependency must be architectural, not contractual — the experience difference must be felt, not licensed

*Collision briefs (now "Auto-Poetic Intelligence Report"):*
• Branded product: "Auto-Poetic Intelligence Report" with sub-tagline "regenerating insight from raw noise"
• Name rooted in autopoiesis (Maturana & Varela, 1972) — self-regenerating intelligence system
• Public-facing artifacts — NEVER expose internal JOTF strategy in briefs
• The brief IS the product — demonstrates collision methodology by doing it
• Archive is the compounding asset, not any single edition

*Lineage:* BioONE (2019) → infoDJ (2020-23) → JOTF (2024-26). Three generations of collision methodology.

*Key rule:* Showing the methodology is fine; explaining the business model behind it is giving away the playbook. Curtis: "this output is the gold, and gives us a lot of flexibility for distribution."

**Why:** Everything ObiWan does in marketing must reinforce this architecture — collision product builds the brand (razor), which creates dependency on InnoGraph (blade).

**How to apply:** When shaping LinkedIn posts, InnoLead placements, or any public content, demonstrate the collision methodology in action but never expose the razor/blade strategy, InnoGraph internals, or PORTHOS collision engine details.
