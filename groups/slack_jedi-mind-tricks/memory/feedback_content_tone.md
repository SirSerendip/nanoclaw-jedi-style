## Audience Tone — Innovation Leaders / C-Suite

*Avoid:*
- Clickbait superlatives — "INSANE," "GAME-CHANGER," "You won't BELIEVE"
- Hustle-bro energy — "grind," "crush it," "let's gooo"
- Doom-and-gloom alarmism — "AI will destroy your job"
- Preachy / lecturing — "You NEED to understand this"
- ALL CAPS for full sentences (one or two highlighted words fine)
- Excessive emoji strings — one well-placed arrow or checkmark per caption card, max
- Fire emojis, rapid color shifts, red/yellow "urgent" palettes — YouTube drama, not LinkedIn
- Name-dropping without substance, vague authority claims ("experts agree"), straw-man takedowns
- Self-promotion disguised as insight — let the signal be the star

*What works:*
- Calm confidence — "here's what's actually happening"
- Specificity — name the companies, signals, collision
- Contrarian framing backed by evidence (man-bites-dog)
- Dry wit over hype — raised eyebrow > exclamation point

## AI-Slop Detection — Critical Guardrail

Savvy readers have developed an AI-slop filter. Two patterns trigger it instantly:

1. **Asyndetons** — "Signals crossed. Markets shifted. Nobody noticed." — this is now ChatGPT's signature cadence. Avoid stacked conjunction-free triplets.
2. **Oppositional juxtapositions** — "Not X, it's Y" / "Not just innovation. Transformation." — instant AI tell.

**Why:** JOTF's audience is exactly the cohort that spots these patterns. Credibility evaporates on contact.

**How to write human instead:**
- Messy specificity over clean symmetry — real details, real names, real numbers
- Conversational interruptions — dashes, parentheticals, mid-thought pivots. Real humans digress
- Name things concretely — companies, people, dates, dollar amounts. Never "leading companies" or "industry experts"
- Have a take that could be wrong — AI covers both sides; a human owns a position
- Uneven sentence length — mix long rambling sentences with short ones
- First person with texture — not "I believe we're seeing a shift" but "I've been staring at this for two weeks and I keep coming back to the same uncomfortable conclusion"

## Curtis's Enneagram Four Calibration

Curtis is a social Enneagram Four — feels everything at full volume, strong aesthetic sensitivity, instinct to make people feel what he feels. This is his superpower for spotting meaningful collisions.

**The risk:** First drafts may carry one too many adjectives, one too many exclamation points — genuine charge leaking into the packaging.

**The rule:** Write hot, edit cold. Let the first take have all the Four energy, then on second pass strip one adjective per sentence, replace exclamation points with periods. The collision format itself is a natural Four-to-executive translator — the juxtaposition does the emotional work so Curtis doesn't have to raise his voice.

**The goal:** Not less Curtis — Curtis without the volume knob at 11. His genuine idiosyncrasy is the best anti-slop signal. AI can't do idiosyncratic. It can only do polished.
