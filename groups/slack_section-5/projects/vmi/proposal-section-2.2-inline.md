# Assessment Architecture

Our assessment strategy integrates three complementary measurement streams into a unified leadership capacity profile for each VMI population segment (cadets, staff, faculty):

1. **BANI+ Psychometric Composite** — a structured survey instrument drawing on validated, peer-reviewed scales mapped to four leadership capacity dimensions
2. **Leadership Circle Profile 360** — a certified multi-rater behavioral assessment administered to leadership cohorts
3. **AI-Augmented Qualitative Analysis** — semantic analysis of structured engagement sessions and situational judgment responses (detailed in Section 2.3)

This integrated architecture satisfies VMI's requirement for a survey-based instrument capable of collecting quantitative data and benchmarking against external datasets, while extending far beyond what any single off-the-shelf instrument can provide.

### The BANI+ Capacity Framework

Our assessment is organized around four leadership capacities identified by futurist Jamais Cascio as essential for leading in today's operating environment. Each capacity directly responds to a condition of the contemporary landscape:

| Condition (BANI) | Capacity (BANI+) | What We Measure |
|---|---|---|
| Brittle — systems that appear strong but shatter under stress | **Bendable** — adaptive resilience | The ability to respond to disruptions of unanticipated intensity, duration, or timing; preparation that transfers across contexts |
| Anxious — pervasive uncertainty that paralyzes action | **Attentive** — empathic awareness | The capacity to recognize and respond to anxiety in oneself and others; creating psychological safety; awareness of the bigger picture |
| Non-linear — disproportionate cause-and-effect | **Neuroflexible** — cognitive agility | The ability to recognize changing conditions and adjust on the fly; abandoning planned responses when conditions demand it |
| Incomprehensible — complexity exceeding individual capacity | **Interconnected** — boundary-spanning intelligence | Communicating across diverse networks; seeking variety of perspective over volume; catalyzing connection across boundaries |

This is not an abstract academic framework. BANI+ describes the exact capacities VMI graduates need — whether they serve in military command, federal agencies, or private sector leadership. The framework gives VMI a developmental language that bridges all post-graduation domains.

### Psychometric Composite Survey

Each BANI+ dimension is measured using validated, peer-reviewed instruments with published normative data. The composite totals approximately 72 items with an estimated completion time of 15-18 minutes — deployable across VMI's full assessment population of approximately 1,750 participants.

All selected instruments are open-access with no per-administration licensing fees, enabling cost-efficient deployment at scale. Every dimension has published external benchmark data, allowing VMI to compare its leadership capacity profile against peer populations. A complete instrument-by-instrument specification with construct rationale, benchmarking sources, and psychometric properties is provided in Appendix X.

The composite survey is delivered through SurvAI, an integrated digital assessment platform that presents the multi-instrument battery as a unified, cohesive participant experience — not as a collection of separate scales.

### Leadership Circle Profile 360

In addition to the self-report composite, our team includes a certified Leadership Circle Profile practitioner (Shana Hall Graves, Soma Triune Consulting). The Leadership Circle Profile is a validated 360-degree assessment that maps leadership competencies across Creative and Reactive dimensions, providing behavioral feedback from peers, direct reports, and supervisors.

The 360 is administered to selected leadership cohorts — not the full 1,750 — providing depth of behavioral insight where the self-report composite provides breadth of population coverage. The two instruments complement each other: the BANI+ composite reveals how individuals *perceive* their own capacities; the 360 reveals how others *experience* their leadership behavior.

This dual-stream approach — broad self-report plus targeted multi-rater — gives VMI both population-level capacity mapping and individual-level developmental feedback within a single integrated assessment architecture.
