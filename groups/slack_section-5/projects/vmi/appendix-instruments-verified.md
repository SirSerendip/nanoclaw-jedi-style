#### Bendable — Adaptive Resilience (17 items)

| Instrument | Items | Published Norms | Construct |
|---|---|---|---|
| [Brief Resilience Scale (BRS) — Smith et al. (2008)](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/18696313/) | 6 | General population, healthcare, military; published cut-off scores | Recovery from stress; resilience under unanticipated conditions |
| [Brief Resilient Coping Scale (BRCS) — Sinclair & Wallston (2004)](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/14994958/) | 4 | General population; [military validation (2024, Military Behavioral Health)](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/21635781.2024.2342257) | Active adaptive coping — "creative ways to alter difficult situations"; preparation that transfers |
| [Acceptance & Action Questionnaire (AAQ-II) — Bond et al. (2011)](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/22035996/) | 7 | Organizational employees + [Special Operations Forces validation](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S2212144722000850) | Psychological flexibility — willingness to change approach rather than rigidly persisting |

**Why these instruments:** Cascio defines Bendable as more than bounce-back. It includes proactive preparation, reimagining how we work, and resilience under novel conditions. The BRS captures recovery capacity, the BRCS captures proactive adaptive coping (the preparation dimension), and the AAQ-II captures the willingness to flex rather than persist rigidly — validated with military special operations populations.

**Benchmarking:** BRS has published population norms (mean 3.70, scale 1-5) with established cut-offs for high/normal/low resilience. AAQ-II has norming across 2,816 participants including both civilian employees and military personnel. BRCS has military-specific validation data published in 2024. All three provide the external benchmark data VMI requires.

#### Attentive — Empathic Awareness (24 items)

| Instrument | Items | Published Norms | Construct |
|---|---|---|---|
| [Interpersonal Reactivity Index (IRI) — Davis (1983), perspective-taking + empathic concern subscales](https://www.eckerd.edu/psychology/iri/) | 14 | Extensively normed across populations worldwide | Perspective-taking and empathic responsiveness — recognizing what others face |
| [Emotion Regulation Questionnaire (ERQ) — Gross & John (2003)](https://spl.stanford.edu/sites/g/files/sbiybj19321/files/media/file/english_0.pdf) | 10 | Broad population norms; hosted openly by Stanford | Self-regulation strategies for managing one's own emotional responses, including anxiety |

**Why these instruments:** Cascio's Attentive is explicitly dual-directional — recognizing anxiety in others *and* managing one's own. The IRI's perspective-taking subscale measures the capacity to see challenges from others' viewpoints, while the empathic concern subscale measures emotional responsiveness. The ERQ captures self-regulation — the "taking care of one's own anxiety and trauma" that Cascio identifies as a precondition for understanding others.

**Benchmarking:** The IRI is one of the most widely used empathy measures in psychology, with extensive published norms across age, gender, and culture. The ERQ has broad normative data available through [Stanford's Psychophysiology Laboratory](https://spl.stanford.edu/).

#### Neuroflexible — Cognitive Agility (19 items + custom SJT)

| Instrument | Items | Published Norms | Construct |
|---|---|---|---|
| [Multiple Stimulus Types Ambiguity Tolerance Scale (MSTAT-II) — McLain (2009)](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/20099561/) | 13 | Organizational populations; reliability α = .83 | Comfort operating in ambiguous, rapidly changing environments |
| [Need for Cognition Scale — short form (NCS-6) — Coelho, Hanel & Wolf (2020)](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7545655/) | 6 | Broad population norms across US/UK samples; CC BY 4.0 open-access | Active engagement with complex thinking rather than defaulting to simple explanations |

**Why these instruments:** Cascio describes Neuroflexible as the capacity to improvise — to recognize that conditions have changed and toss out planned responses. The MSTAT-II measures tolerance of the ambiguity that characterizes non-linear environments. The NCS-6 measures the cognitive drive to engage with complexity rather than simplify prematurely, which is the mental precondition for the kind of real-time reframing Cascio describes. (The NCS-6 is a psychometrically optimized short form derived from Cacioppo & Petty's original 18-item Need for Cognition Scale, with confirmed measurement invariance across populations.)

**The gap — and our innovation:** No existing validated instrument measures the specific capacity Cascio highlights through his Stanislav Petrov example: the willingness to break from one's own script when anomalous patterns emerge under pressure. This is not a limitation — it is where our AI-augmented qualitative analysis provides measurement that no off-the-shelf instrument can (see Section 2.3).

**Benchmarking:** MSTAT-II has published organizational norms (McLain, 2009, *Psychological Reports*). NCS-6 has published norms across US/UK populations with confirmed measurement invariance (Coelho et al., 2020, *Assessment*).

#### Interconnected — Boundary-Spanning Intelligence (19 items)

| Instrument | Items | Published Norms | Construct |
|---|---|---|---|
| [Top Management Team Boundary-Spanning Leadership Scale (TMTBSL) — Liu et al. (2022)](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/psychology/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2022.988771/full) | 14 | Organizational populations; peer-reviewed CFA validation (Frontiers in Psychology, 2022) | Boundary-spanning leadership — foresight, integration, inspiring connection across silos |
| [Ancona & Caldwell Boundary Spanning Scale, representation subscale (1992)](https://web.mit.edu/curhan/www/docs/Articles/15341_Readings/Group_Dynamics/Ancona_Caldwell_1992_Bridging_the_boundary.pdf) | 5 | US organizational teams; foundational research (Administrative Science Quarterly, 1992; 2,200+ citations) | Active boundary spanning behavior — reaching across group boundaries to access diverse perspectives |

**Why these instruments:** Cascio's Interconnected is about variety, not volume — seeking out diverse minds and weaving them together. The TMTBSL directly measures boundary-spanning leadership behaviors including integration and foresight. Ancona & Caldwell's foundational representation subscale captures the behavioral act of spanning boundaries to access external perspectives and resources.

**The gap — and our innovation:** No existing instrument measures what we call *network diversity seeking* — the active preference for perspective variety over network size, and the catalyst behavior of intentionally connecting unlike minds. This is addressed through our AI-augmented qualitative analysis (see Section 2.3).

**Benchmarking:** TMTBSL has published validation data with factor loadings and descriptive statistics (open-access via Frontiers in Psychology). Ancona & Caldwell's foundational 1992 paper has been cited in over 2,200 subsequent studies with extensive comparative data.

#### Shared: Psychological Safety (7 items)

| Instrument | Items | Published Norms | Construct |
|---|---|---|---|
| [Team Psychological Safety Scale — Edmondson (1999)](https://web.mit.edu/curhan/www/docs/Articles/15341_Readings/Group_Performance/Edmondson%20Psychological%20safety.pdf) | 7 | Broad organizational norms; construct independently validated by [Google's Project Aristotle](https://rework.withgoogle.com/print/guides/5721312655835136/) as the #1 predictor of team effectiveness | The felt safety to take interpersonal risks — prerequisite for both Attentive (creating safety) and Interconnected (spanning boundaries) |

This scale scores to both the Attentive and Interconnected dimensions, administered once. Psychological safety is the enabling condition without which neither empathic engagement nor boundary-spanning behavior can occur.
