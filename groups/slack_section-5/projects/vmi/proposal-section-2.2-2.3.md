## 2.2 Assessment Architecture

### Overview

Our assessment strategy integrates three complementary measurement streams into a unified leadership capacity profile for each VMI population segment (cadets, staff, faculty):

1. **BANI+ Psychometric Composite** — a structured survey instrument drawing on validated, peer-reviewed scales mapped to four leadership capacity dimensions
2. **Leadership Circle Profile 360** — a certified multi-rater behavioral assessment administered to leadership cohorts
3. **AI-Augmented Qualitative Analysis** — semantic analysis of structured engagement sessions and situational judgment responses (detailed in Section 2.3)

This integrated architecture satisfies VMI's requirement for a survey-based instrument capable of collecting quantitative data and benchmarking against external datasets, while extending far beyond what any single off-the-shelf instrument can provide.

### The BANI+ Capacity Framework

Our assessment is organized around four leadership capacities identified by futurist Jamais Cascio as essential for leading in today's operating environment. Each capacity directly responds to a condition of the contemporary landscape:

| Condition (BANI) | Capacity (BANI+) | What We Measure |
|---|---|---|
| Brittle — systems that appear strong but shatter under stress | **Bendable** — adaptive resilience | The ability to respond to disruptions of unanticipated intensity, duration, or timing; preparation that transfers across contexts |
| Anxious — pervasive uncertainty that paralyzes action | **Attentive** — empathic awareness | The capacity to recognize and respond to anxiety in oneself and others; creating psychological safety; awareness of the bigger picture |
| Non-linear — disproportionate cause-and-effect | **Neuroflexible** — cognitive agility | The ability to recognize changing conditions and adjust on the fly; abandoning planned responses when conditions demand it |
| Incomprehensible — complexity exceeding individual capacity | **Interconnected** — boundary-spanning intelligence | Communicating across diverse networks; seeking variety of perspective over volume; catalyzing connection across boundaries |

This is not an abstract academic framework. BANI+ describes the exact capacities VMI graduates need — whether they serve in military command, federal agencies, or private sector leadership. The framework gives VMI a developmental language that bridges all post-graduation domains.

### Psychometric Composite Survey (~72 items)

Each BANI+ dimension is measured using validated, peer-reviewed instruments with published normative data. All selected instruments are open-access — no per-administration licensing fees — enabling cost-efficient deployment across VMI's full assessment population of approximately 1,750 participants.

#### Bendable — Adaptive Resilience (17 items)

| Instrument | Items | Published Norms | Construct |
|---|---|---|---|
| Brief Resilience Scale (BRS) — Smith et al. | 6 | General population, healthcare, military; published cut-off scores | Recovery from stress; resilience under unanticipated conditions |
| Brief Resilient Coping Scale (BRCS) — Sinclair & Wallston | 4 | General population; military validation (2024, Military Behavioral Health) | Active adaptive coping — "creative ways to alter difficult situations"; preparation that transfers |
| Acceptance & Action Questionnaire (AAQ-II) — Bond et al. | 7 | Organizational employees + Special Operations Forces | Psychological flexibility — willingness to change approach rather than rigidly persisting |

**Why these instruments:** Cascio defines Bendable as more than bounce-back. It includes proactive preparation, reimagining how we work, and resilience under novel conditions. The BRS captures recovery capacity, the BRCS captures proactive adaptive coping (the preparation dimension), and the AAQ-II captures the willingness to flex rather than persist rigidly — validated with military special operations populations.

**Benchmarking:** BRS has published population norms (mean 3.70, scale 1-5) with established cut-offs for high/normal/low resilience. AAQ-II has norming across 2,816 participants including both civilian employees and military personnel. BRCS has military-specific validation data published in 2024. All three provide the external benchmark data VMI requires.

#### Attentive — Empathic Awareness (24 items)

| Instrument | Items | Published Norms | Construct |
|---|---|---|---|
| Interpersonal Reactivity Index (IRI) — Davis (perspective-taking + empathic concern subscales) | 14 | Extensively normed across populations worldwide | Perspective-taking and empathic responsiveness — recognizing what others face |
| Emotion Regulation Questionnaire (ERQ) — Gross & John | 10 | Broad population norms; hosted openly by Stanford | Self-regulation strategies for managing one's own emotional responses, including anxiety |

**Why these instruments:** Cascio's Attentive is explicitly dual-directional — recognizing anxiety in others *and* managing one's own. The IRI's perspective-taking subscale measures the capacity to see challenges from others' viewpoints, while the empathic concern subscale measures emotional responsiveness. The ERQ captures self-regulation — the "taking care of one's own anxiety and trauma" that Cascio identifies as a precondition for understanding others.

**Benchmarking:** The IRI is one of the most widely used empathy measures in psychology, with extensive published norms across age, gender, and culture. The ERQ has broad normative data available through Stanford's Psychophysiology Laboratory.

#### Neuroflexible — Cognitive Agility (19 items + custom SJT)

| Instrument | Items | Published Norms | Construct |
|---|---|---|---|
| Multiple Stimulus Types Ambiguity Tolerance Scale (MSTAT-II) — McLain | 13 | Organizational populations; reliability α = .83 | Comfort operating in ambiguous, rapidly changing environments |
| Need for Cognition Scale — short form (NCS-6) — Coelho, Hanel & Wolf | 6 | Broad population norms across multiple studies; CC BY 4.0 open-access | Active engagement with complex thinking rather than defaulting to simple explanations |

**Why these instruments:** Cascio describes Neuroflexible as the capacity to improvise — to recognize that conditions have changed and toss out planned responses. The MSTAT-II measures tolerance of the ambiguity that characterizes non-linear environments. The NCS-6 measures the cognitive drive to engage with complexity rather than simplify prematurely, which is the mental precondition for the kind of real-time reframing Cascio describes.

**The gap — and our innovation:** No existing validated instrument measures the specific capacity Cascio highlights through his Stanislav Petrov example: the willingness to break from one's own script when anomalous patterns emerge under pressure. This is not a limitation — it is where our AI-augmented qualitative analysis provides measurement that no off-the-shelf instrument can (see Section 2.3).

**Benchmarking:** MSTAT-II has published organizational norms. NCS-6 has extensive published data across populations.

#### Interconnected — Boundary-Spanning Intelligence (19 items)

| Instrument | Items | Published Norms | Construct |
|---|---|---|---|
| Top Management Team Boundary-Spanning Leadership Scale (TMTBSL) | 14 | Organizational populations; peer-reviewed CFA validation (Frontiers in Psychology, 2022) | Boundary-spanning leadership — foresight, integration, inspiring connection across silos |
| Ancona & Caldwell Boundary Spanning Scale | 5 | US organizational teams; foundational research (Administrative Science Quarterly, 1992) | Active boundary spanning behavior — reaching across group boundaries to access diverse perspectives |

**Why these instruments:** Cascio's Interconnected is about variety, not volume — seeking out diverse minds and weaving them together. The TMTBSL directly measures boundary-spanning leadership behaviors including integration and foresight. Ancona & Caldwell's foundational scale captures the behavioral act of spanning boundaries to access external perspectives and resources.

**The gap — and our innovation:** No existing instrument measures what we call *network diversity seeking* — the active preference for perspective variety over network size, and the catalyst behavior of intentionally connecting unlike minds. This is addressed through our AI-augmented qualitative analysis (see Section 2.3).

**Benchmarking:** TMTBSL has published validation data with factor loadings and descriptive statistics. Ancona & Caldwell's scale has been cited in over 2,000 subsequent studies with extensive comparative data.

#### Shared: Psychological Safety (7 items)

| Instrument | Items | Published Norms | Construct |
|---|---|---|---|
| Team Psychological Safety Scale — Edmondson | 7 | Broad organizational norms; Google Project Aristotle benchmarks | The felt safety to take interpersonal risks — prerequisite for both Attentive (creating safety) and Interconnected (spanning boundaries) |

This scale scores to both the Attentive and Interconnected dimensions, administered once. Psychological safety is the enabling condition without which neither empathic engagement nor boundary-spanning behavior can occur.

**Estimated completion time:** 15-18 minutes. Deployable across all 1,750 participants (approximately 1,500 cadets and 250 staff members) without per-administration cost constraints.

**Delivery platform:** The composite survey is delivered through SurvAI, an integrated digital assessment platform that presents the multi-instrument battery as a unified, cohesive participant experience — not as a collection of separate scales. Respondents experience a single assessment with consistent design language, not an obvious patchwork of different instruments. SurvAI handles deployment, data collection, scoring, and initial analytics.

### Leadership Circle Profile 360

In addition to the self-report composite, our team includes a certified Leadership Circle Profile practitioner (Shana Hall Graves, Soma Triune Consulting). The Leadership Circle Profile is a validated 360-degree assessment that maps leadership competencies across Creative and Reactive dimensions, providing behavioral feedback from peers, direct reports, and supervisors.

The 360 is administered to selected leadership cohorts — not the full 1,750 — providing depth of behavioral insight where the self-report composite provides breadth of population coverage. The two instruments complement each other: the BANI+ composite reveals how individuals *perceive* their own capacities; the 360 reveals how others *experience* their leadership behavior.

This dual-stream approach — broad self-report plus targeted multi-rater — gives VMI both population-level capacity mapping and individual-level developmental feedback within a single integrated assessment architecture.

## 2.3 AI-Augmented Qualitative Analysis

### The Measurement Problem We Solve

Validated psychometric instruments excel at measuring well-established constructs. For four of six target capacities — resilience, empathy, ambiguity tolerance, and boundary-spanning behavior — excellent instruments exist with published norms. We use them.

But two critical capacities have no validated self-report measure:

1. **Script-breaking under pressure** — the willingness to abandon established protocol when anomalous patterns emerge in high-stakes conditions (Cascio's Neuroflexible, illustrated by Stanislav Petrov's 1983 decision)
2. **Network diversity seeking** — the active preference for perspective variety over network volume, and the catalyst behavior of intentionally connecting unlike minds (Cascio's Interconnected, illustrated by Dr. Kizzmekia Corbett's cross-disciplinary team assembly)

These are arguably the two most important capacities for VMI's mission — and they cannot be reliably measured by asking someone to rate themselves on a 1-5 scale. People who genuinely break scripts under pressure often don't recognize it as a skill. People who naturally weave diverse networks don't think of it as a competency. Self-report is the wrong modality for these constructs.

We propose a different approach.

### Three-Stream Qualitative Intelligence

#### Stream 1: Situational Judgment Analysis

We develop 3-5 situational judgment scenarios grounded in OODA theory and tailored to VMI's military leadership context. Each scenario presents a realistic decision point where the "right" response requires breaking from established procedure based on anomalous pattern recognition.

*Example scenario structure:*
> You are leading a project team executing a well-established protocol. Midway through execution, you notice data patterns that don't match what the protocol assumes. Your team is performing well by all standard metrics. The protocol has worked reliably in the past. What do you do?

Unlike traditional SJTs that score for a single "correct" answer, our analysis examines the *reasoning architecture* of responses:

- Does the respondent reference the anomalous data or default to protocol compliance?
- Do they generate alternative explanations or seek additional information?
- Do they distinguish between "the protocol is working" and "the protocol is appropriate"?
- Do they consider who else should be consulted or informed?

Situational judgment methodology has strong predictive validity in military selection research, and the format will feel natural to VMI cadets who are already immersed in scenario-based training environments.

#### Stream 2: Engagement Session Transcript Analysis

The RFP requires structured engagement sessions and focus groups with cadets, staff, and faculty. These sessions generate rich qualitative data that is typically reduced to thematic summaries and illustrative quotes — discarding most of the signal.

We apply semantic algorithmic analysis to engagement session transcripts to detect demonstrated capacity that participants may not self-report:

**For Script-Breaking (Neuroflexible):**
- Frequency and quality of reframing — does the participant challenge the premise of questions or accept the frame as given?
- Reference to multiple mental models when discussing decision-making
- Language patterns that indicate comfort with "I was wrong" or "I changed my approach when I noticed..."
- Spontaneous generation of alternative explanations versus single-cause attributions

**For Network Diversity Seeking (Interconnected):**
- Breadth of reference — does the participant draw examples from multiple domains or stay within their immediate context?
- Bridging language — connecting ideas, people, or fields that others treat as separate
- Frequency of "have you talked to..." or "the people in [different unit/department] see it differently" patterns
- Evidence of actively seeking disconfirming perspectives versus reinforcing existing views

This analysis does not replace human interpretation. Our team conducts the engagement sessions, reads the transcripts, and develops the qualitative findings. The AI-augmented layer adds pattern detection at a scale and consistency that human reading alone cannot achieve — particularly when processing sessions across 1,750 participants over multiple engagement rounds.

#### Stream 3: Quantitative-Qualitative Cross-Referencing

The most powerful analytical move is not either stream in isolation — it is the *integration* of both.

The BANI+ composite survey tells us how individuals perceive their own capacities (self-report). The transcript analysis tells us how those capacities show up in their actual language and reasoning (demonstrated behavior). The delta between the two is itself diagnostic:

| Self-Report | Demonstrated | Interpretation | Development Implication |
|---|---|---|---|
| High flexibility | High flexibility | Accurate self-awareness + strong capacity | Leverage and develop as peer leaders |
| High flexibility | Low flexibility | Over-estimation or aspirational self-image | Targeted coaching on self-awareness |
| Low flexibility | High flexibility | Under-recognition of own strength | Build confidence; these are hidden assets |
| Low flexibility | Low flexibility | Accurate awareness of growth area | Structured developmental intervention |

This matrix applies across all BANI+ dimensions and provides VMI with a nuanced, four-quadrant view of its leadership capacity landscape — not just a set of scores, but a map of where self-perception aligns with and diverges from demonstrated behavior.

### Human-Driven, AI-Supported

In accordance with VMI's General Order 50 and the expectations stated in Addendum 1 (Question 19), all analysis and interpretation is driven by human expertise:

- Engagement sessions are designed, facilitated, and interpreted by our team's experienced consultants
- AI-augmented analysis provides pattern detection and consistency at scale
- All findings, conclusions, and recommendations are the product of human professional judgment
- The AI layer augments our team's capacity to process qualitative data across a large population — it does not replace the clinical and contextual judgment that only experienced practitioners can provide

### Compounding Intelligence Across OODA Cycles

This analytical architecture becomes more powerful with each cycle of the multi-year engagement:

- **Year 1:** Baseline assessment — first full dataset establishes capacity profiles and calibrates the analytical models
- **Year 2:** Comparative analysis — incoming class data benchmarked against Year 1; longitudinal tracking begins for returning cohorts; the system now has comparison data
- **Year 3:** Trajectory detection — enough longitudinal data to identify developmental trajectories and predict which interventions are driving measurable change
- **Year 4:** Institutional intelligence — four years of integrated quantitative and qualitative data provides VMI with a leadership development knowledge base no single-year assessment could produce

Each OODA cycle generates new observable data that sharpens the next cycle's orientation. This is Boyd's core insight applied to institutional development: the organization that cycles through observe-orient-decide-act faster and with higher fidelity develops a decisive advantage. By Year 4, VMI's leadership development infrastructure operates with a depth of self-knowledge that peer institutions — relying on periodic surveys and anecdotal feedback — cannot match.

### Why This Matters for VMI

No other bidder will propose this capability. Traditional consulting responses will offer an off-the-shelf survey, a report, and a set of recommendations. Our approach integrates:

- Validated psychometric measurement (the science)
- AI-augmented qualitative analysis (the technology)
- OODA-structured developmental cycling (the methodology)
- BANI+ capacity framing (the interpretive lens)

into a unified system where each component strengthens the others. The assessment *is* the intervention — the act of measuring leadership capacity through this framework begins developing the very capacities it measures. Cadets who engage with situational judgment scenarios about script-breaking are already practicing Neuroflexibility. Staff who participate in engagement sessions where their boundary-spanning language is analyzed are already exercising Interconnection.

This is not assessment followed by development. It is assessment *as* development — the first turn of the OODA loop that will accelerate across four years of partnership with VMI.
