# Psychometric Instrument Mapping: BANI+ Dimensions

> Research synthesis — validated instruments mapped to each BANI+ capacity.
> Focus: licensing accessibility, construct fit, brevity, benchmark availability.
> Conducted: 2026-04-16

---

## Executive Summary

Across 40+ instruments researched, a viable all-open-access composite emerges for each BANI+ dimension. Total recommended battery: ~55-65 items (plus Leadership Circle Profile 360 administered separately by Shana). No per-administration licensing fees required for the core composite.

One significant gap: no existing validated instrument directly measures the "Petrov move" — willingness to abandon protocol under anomalous pattern recognition. This is the sharpest construct in Neuroflexible and may warrant 3-5 custom situational judgment items.

## BENDABLE (Adaptive Resilience)

### Recommended Primary Instruments

| Instrument | Items | Licensing | Construct Focus | Norms |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Brief Resilience Scale (BRS)** | 6 | OPEN — items published, free | Bounce-back resilience | General pop, healthcare, military; mean 3.70, published cut-offs |
| **AAQ-II** | 7 | FREE for research/clinical; permission for commercial | Psychological flexibility | Org + SOF military; mean 18.0 (SD 6.4) |
| **Brief Resilient Coping Scale (BRCS)** | 4 | OPEN — items published | Active adaptive coping, "creative ways to alter difficult situations" | General pop, military (2024 validation) |
| **Adaptive Leadership Behavior Scale (ALBS)** | 15 | OPEN ACCESS (CC BY) | Adaptive leadership behavior | Organizational (Dutch); peer-reviewed Frontiers 2023 |

### Recommended Composite: 32 items
BRS (6) + AAQ-II (7) + BRCS (4) + ALBS (15) = **32 items, $0 licensing**

### Why These

- BRS captures resilience recovery (Cascio's "respond to stressful dynamics")
- AAQ-II captures psychological flexibility (willingness to change approach)
- BRCS captures proactive adaptive coping ("preparation that transfers across contexts")
- ALBS captures the leadership-behavioral expression of bendability
- All four have published norms; three have military or organizational validation

### Considered & Set Aside

| Instrument | Items | Why Not |
|---|---|---|
| CD-RISC 10/25 | 10/25 | Gold standard but PROPRIETARY — opaque per-case pricing |
| PsyCap (PCQ) | 24 | PROPRIETARY via Mind Garden ($1-3/admin); resilience is only 1 of 4 subscales |
| Cognitive Flexibility Scale (CFS) | 12 | Open and free but normed only on undergrad comm students; weak org norms |

## ATTENTIVE (Empathic Awareness + Psychological Safety)

### Recommended Primary Instruments

| Instrument | Items | Licensing | Construct Focus | Norms |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Interpersonal Reactivity Index (IRI)** | 14 (2 subscales) | OPEN — items published | Perspective-taking + empathic concern | Extensively normed across populations |
| **Psychological Safety Scale** | 7 | OPEN — items in Edmondson 1999 | Creating safety for others | Corporate, healthcare, education, military; Google Project Aristotle benchmarks |
| **Emotion Regulation Questionnaire (ERQ)** | 10 | FREE — Stanford hosts openly | Self-regulation of anxiety | Broad norms, multiple populations |
| **Langer Mindfulness Scale (LMS)** | 14 (short) | OPEN — items published | Active mindfulness (not meditation-based) | Organizational, educational |

### Recommended Composite: ~45 items
IRI perspective-taking + empathic concern (14) + Edmondson TPS (7) + ERQ (10) + LMS short form (14) = **~45 items, $0 licensing**

### Why These

- IRI is the strongest single match for Cascio's "recognize and respond to anxiety in yourself and others"
- Edmondson TPS directly measures the capacity to *create* psychological safety — not just experience it
- ERQ captures self-regulation of own anxiety (Cascio: "taking care of one's own anxiety and trauma")
- LMS measures active/engaged mindfulness (awareness of bigger picture) vs. meditative mindfulness
- All are open-access with published items and published norms

### Considered & Set Aside

| Instrument | Items | Why Not |
|---|---|---|
| MAAS | 15 | Measures trait mindfulness (passive awareness); Cascio's Attentive is more active/empathic |
| FFMQ | 39 | Too long for composite; short form (FFMQ-15, CC BY) viable as alternative to LMS |
| SART | varies | Military situational awareness — good fit but designed for operational/aviation contexts, not leadership development |
| TEQ | 16 | Requires written permission from developer; IRI covers same construct with better norms |

## NEUROFLEXIBLE (Cognitive Agility + Script-Breaking)

### Recommended Primary Instruments

| Instrument | Items | Licensing | Construct Focus | Norms |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Cognitive Flexibility Scale (CFS)** | 12 | OPEN — PsyToolkit hosted | Awareness of alternatives, willingness to flex | University students (mean ~55) |
| **MSTAT-II (McLain)** | 13 | EFFECTIVELY OPEN — items in article | Tolerance of ambiguity | Organizational; alpha .83 |
| **Need for Cognition Scale (NCS-6)** | 6 | FREE for nonprofit | Engagement with complex thinking | Broad norms; excellent efficiency |
| **CFI Alternatives subscale** | ~10 | OPEN — items in article | Perceiving multiple alternative explanations/solutions | Clinical + higher-ed |

### Recommended Composite: ~41 items
CFS (12) + MSTAT-II (13) + NCS-6 (6) + CFI-Alternatives (~10) = **~41 items, $0 licensing**

### Why These

- CFS captures behavioral flexibility and awareness of alternatives (Cascio's "keep alternative approaches in mind")
- MSTAT-II captures comfort operating in ambiguous/chaotic environments
- NCS-6 captures the drive to engage with complexity rather than simplify prematurely
- CFI-Alternatives captures the ability to generate multiple framings of a situation

### The Gap: No "Petrov Move" Instrument

No existing validated self-report instrument measures the specific construct of *willingness to abandon one's own protocol when anomalous patterns emerge under pressure*. The closest proxies:

- **I-ADAPT-M** (Ployhart & Bliese): 55-item, 8-dimension individual adaptability measure with crisis, stress, uncertainty, and creative subscales. Best construct match but gray-area licensing and very long.
- **Adaptive Performance Scale** (Charbonnier-Voirin): 19 items, open access on HAL-SHS. Measures adaptive performance behaviors. French origin with organizational validation.

**Recommendation:** Develop 3-5 custom Situational Judgment Test (SJT) items presenting scenarios where the "right" answer requires breaking from established procedure based on anomalous pattern recognition. These can be grounded in the OODA Orient phase literature and VMI's military decision-making context. SJTs have strong predictive validity in military selection research and would resonate culturally with VMI evaluators.

### Considered & Set Aside

| Instrument | Items | Why Not |
|---|---|---|
| Budner Tolerance of Ambiguity | 16 | Weak psychometrics (alpha .49-.68); MSTAT-II is the successor |
| ACS (Fletcher) | 28 | Published openly but measures attributional complexity — weak Neuroflexible fit |
| TALENTx7 / Burke BLAI | varies | Commercial/proprietary ($200-250+/person) |
| Pulakos JAI | 132 | Military-normed but unwieldy; restricted access |

## INTERCONNECTED (Boundary Spanning + Network Diversity)

### Recommended Primary Instruments

| Instrument | Items | Licensing | Construct Focus | Norms |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **TMTBSL (Boundary-Spanning Leadership)** | 14 | OPEN ACCESS — Frontiers 2022 | Foresight, inspiration, integration, resilience, decisiveness | Chinese managers; peer-reviewed CFA |
| **Ancona & Caldwell Boundary Spanning** | 5 | OPEN — items in ASQ 1992 | External boundary spanning behavior | US product development teams |
| **Edmondson TPS-7** | 7 | OPEN — items published | Psychological safety (enabler of interconnection) | Broad organizational norms |
| **3CI subscales** (Willingness to Engage + Cognitive Flexibility) | ~20 | PUBLIC DOMAIN (DTIC) | Cross-boundary engagement, openness | US military (all branches) |

### Recommended Composite: ~46 items
TMTBSL (14) + Ancona-Caldwell (5) + TPS-7 (7) + 3CI selected subscales (~20) = **~46 items, $0 licensing**

Note: TPS-7 appears in both Attentive and Interconnected composites — administer once, score for both.

### Why These

- TMTBSL directly measures boundary-spanning leadership — closest operationalization of Cascio's "weaving together diverse fields"
- Ancona-Caldwell captures active boundary spanning at the team level
- TPS-7 measures the enabling condition — people won't span boundaries without safety
- 3CI "Willingness to Engage" captures the military-relevant dimension of seeking out diverse perspectives

### The Gap: Network Diversity Seeking

No validated instrument specifically measures *preference for perspective variety over network volume* or *catalyst/connector identity* (the Corbett behavior). This is a genuine gap in the literature.

**Recommendation:** 3-5 custom items measuring:
- Active seeking of unlike perspectives when forming teams or solving problems
- Comfort serving as a bridge between groups with different expertise/culture
- Tendency to bring disconnected people together

These can be grounded in Burt's structural holes theory and Granovetter's weak ties literature, both well-established in organizational network research.

### Considered & Set Aside

| Instrument | Items | Why Not |
|---|---|---|
| Relational Coordination Survey | 7/dyad | PROPRIETARY (Brandeis, $500-3,600); measures coordination quality, not diversity-seeking |
| ESCI (Boyatzis/Goleman) | 68 | PROPRIETARY (Korn Ferry, ~$500+/person); too broad; 360-format adds complexity |
| IDI (Hammer) | 50 | PROPRIETARY ($5-20/survey + QA certification); developmental stage model, not behavioral |
| ASI (Lipman-Blumen) | 45 | PROPRIETARY; conceptually excellent fit but locked behind licensing |
| Systems Thinking Scale | 20 | Semi-open but normed only on healthcare; measures systems thinking, not connector behavior |

## Composite Survey Architecture

### Total Item Counts (Recommended Battery)

| BANI+ Dimension | Items | All Open Access |
|---|---|---|
| Bendable | 32 | Yes |
| Attentive | 45 | Yes |
| Neuroflexible | 41 + 3-5 custom SJT | Yes (custom items need piloting) |
| Interconnected | 46 | Yes |
| **Overlap** (TPS-7 shared) | -7 | — |
| **TOTAL** | **~160 + custom items** | — |

### The Problem: 160 Items Is Too Many

At ~1,750 respondents (1,500 cadets + 250 staff), a 160-item survey is a compliance risk. Target: 60-80 items for a 15-20 minute completion time.

### Compression Strategy

| Approach | Result |
|---|---|
| Use short forms where available | Saves ~30 items |
| Select 2 instruments per dimension (primary + supporting) instead of 4 | Saves ~60 items |
| Custom SJT items serve double duty (Neuroflexible + Bendable) | Saves ~5 items |
| Shared instruments across dimensions (TPS-7) | Saves 7 items |

### Lean Composite (~65 items)

| Dimension | Instruments | Items |
|---|---|---|
| Bendable | BRS (6) + BRCS (4) + AAQ-II (7) | 17 |
| Attentive | IRI-PT+EC (14) + ERQ (10) | 24 |
| Neuroflexible | MSTAT-II (13) + NCS-6 (6) | 19 |
| Interconnected | TMTBSL (14) + Ancona-Caldwell (5) | 19 |
| Shared | TPS-7 (scores to Attentive + Interconnected) | 7 |
| Custom | SJT items (Neuroflexible script-breaking) | 3-5 |
| **TOTAL** | | **~72-74 items** |

Estimated completion time: 15-18 minutes. Acceptable for cadet population.

### Supplementary: Leadership Circle Profile 360

Administered separately by Shana Hall Graves (certified practitioner) to leadership cohorts — not the full 1,750. Provides 360-degree behavioral feedback that the self-report composite cannot. Covers relational awareness, creative competencies, and reactive tendencies. Proprietary but already in-hand as a team asset.

## Licensing Risk Summary

| Risk | Instruments | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| None — fully open | BRS, BRCS, CFS, MSTAT-II, CFI, TPS-7, TMTBSL, Ancona-Caldwell, 3CI, LMS | Items published in peer-reviewed articles |
| Low — free with attribution | AAQ-II, ERQ, NCS-6 | Free for research; commercial use needs author permission (typically granted) |
| Medium — permission required | IRI | Widely used without formal permission; items published openly; Davis has not restricted use |
| Avoided | CD-RISC, PCQ, ESCI, IDI, ASI, RC Survey | Proprietary licensing incompatible with $40K budget |

### Budget Impact: $0 for instruments

All recommended instruments are free. The only psychometric cost is Shana's Leadership Circle Profile 360 delivery, which is an existing team capability.

## Gaps Requiring Custom Development

1. **Script-breaking under pressure** (Neuroflexible) — 3-5 SJT items
2. **Network diversity seeking / catalyst identity** (Interconnected) — 3-5 self-report items

Total custom items: 6-10. These should be:
- Developed during the OBSERVE phase (Year 1)
- Piloted with a small VMI cohort before full deployment
- Grounded in published theory (Boyd/OODA for SJT; Burt/Granovetter for network items)
- Reviewed with VMI Office of Assessment & Institutional Research (per Addendum Q1, Q26)

This is not a psychometric weakness — it's a *differentiator*. The proposal can position these custom items as purpose-built for VMI's military leadership context, something no off-the-shelf instrument provides.
