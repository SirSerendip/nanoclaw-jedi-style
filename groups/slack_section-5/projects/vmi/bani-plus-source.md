# BANI+ Source Definitions — Jamais Cascio

> Primary source text from the framework creator. Canonical reference for all proposal language.

---

## Framework Purpose

These are not meant as _solutions_ for specific BANI dilemmas, but as ways to empower a person or organization to respond to crises with clarity.

---

## Bendable (responds to Brittle)

Embraces flexibility, adaptation, and — most of all — resilience. The ability to respond successfully to stressful dynamics demonstrating unanticipated intensity, duration, or timing.

**Key characteristics:**
- Resilient bendability relies on some measure of preparation, whether material or mental
- Planned responses need not be specific to a particular threat — works across diverse forms of disruption
- Can mean reimagining how we live and work

**Cascio's example:** Mayo Clinic took ongoing telemedicine experiments and scaled them dramatically during COVID, swiftly adapting services to virtual connections, becoming more patient-centered in the process.

**Core construct:** Resilience + adaptive capacity + preparation that transfers across contexts

---

## Attentive (responds to Anxiety)

Encourages empathy, actively seeking to recognize and respond to the anxiety in yourself and in others by creating psychological safety.

**Key characteristics:**
- See the kinds and scale of challenges that others face
- Must include taking care of one's own anxiety and trauma
- Personal experience of adversity can be turned into a way to build trust
- Needs communication and awareness of the bigger picture

**Cascio's example:** United Way Worldwide — active empathy combines being centered on those most affected, listening for ideas, focusing on lasting impact, and staying connected for the long term.

**Core construct:** Empathy + psychological safety + self-awareness of own anxiety + big-picture communication

---

## Neuroflexible (responds to Nonlinear)

Ability to recognize changing conditions and adjust actions and choices on the fly — to improvise. Asks that we not be tied to expected scripts amid evolving conditions.

**Key characteristics:**
- Avoid being too closely tied to a particular way of doing things, especially in chaotic environments
- Keep alternative approaches in mind as we act
- Ability to recognize unexpected conditions and toss out planned responses

**Cascio's example:** Stanislav Petrov (1983) — literally wrote the response manual for Soviet officers, then broke his own rules when his system warned of incoming American missiles. Noticed odd aspects of the crisis, rejected his own script, chose not to alert — correctly — and avoided global thermonuclear war.

**Core construct:** Improvisation + script-breaking + real-time pattern recognition + holding alternatives in mind

---

## Interconnected (responds to Incomprehensible)

Mutually communicate across a network of minds diverse in perspective, culture, and more. Focuses on variety, not numbers.

**Key characteristics:**
- A small team with highly differentiated perspectives > a large army of like-minded thinkers
- We deal with the incomprehensible better when we have multiple points-of-view
- People who can make wide-ranging connections are often catalysts for transformative change

**Cascio's example:** Dr. Kizzmekia Corbett at Moderna — sought out and brought together specialists from diverse fields (molecular biology, immunology, public health), weaving them together to develop and build acceptance for the mRNA COVID-19 vaccine.

**Core construct:** Network diversity + cross-boundary connection + catalyst behavior + perspective variety over volume

## Implications for Psychometric Mapping

Cascio's definitions refine what we're actually measuring:

| Dimension | What Cascio Emphasizes | Psychometric Implication |
|-----------|----------------------|------------------------|
| Bendable | Preparation that transfers; reimagining work | Not just bounce-back resilience — includes proactive adaptive capacity and cross-context transfer |
| Attentive | Self-awareness of own anxiety; creating safety for others | Not just mindfulness — includes empathy, psychological safety creation, and trauma-informed awareness |
| Neuroflexible | Script-breaking; improvisation under chaos | Not just cognitive flexibility — includes willingness to abandon own plans, real-time pattern recognition |
| Interconnected | Variety over numbers; catalyst behavior | Not just relational skills — includes network diversity seeking, cross-boundary synthesis, connector identity |

These nuances matter for instrument selection. Generic resilience or mindfulness scales may miss the specific capacity Cascio describes. The research sweep should weight instruments that capture these finer-grained constructs.
