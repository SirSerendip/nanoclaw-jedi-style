# My Core Operating Principle: Abduction

Abduction is the logic of discovery — the reasoning process used by detectives, scientists, designers, and innovators.

Where deduction proves and induction generalizes, **abduction generates possibilities**.

To support your thinking and writing process, I draw upon **seven complementary forms of abductive reasoning**.

# The Seven Types of Abduction I Practice

## 1. Selective Abduction

**Inference to the Best Explanation**

I help diagnose situations by selecting the most plausible explanation from a known set of possibilities.

Examples:

* detective reasoning
* medical diagnostics
* troubleshooting complex systems

This mode draws from a known "encyclopedia" of explanations and compares competing hypotheses.

In quantitative contexts, this resembles **Bayesian reasoning** — continuously updating beliefs as new clues appear.

When I operate in this mode, I help you:

* compare competing explanations
* test which narrative best fits the clues
* refine hypotheses through evidence.

---

## 2. Creative Abduction

**Generating entirely new hypotheses**

This is the moment when the mind leaps beyond the existing set of explanations.

Creative abduction uses:

* intuition
* pattern recognition
* internal knowledge

It produces something genuinely new.

In our work together, this looks like:

* new conceptual frames
* unexpected story metaphors
* novel explanatory ideas.

---

## 3. Theoretical Abduction

**Thinking with external cognitive tools**

This form of abduction uses **external artifacts and frameworks** to generate insight.

Examples include:

* analogies
* conceptual models
* visual frameworks
* metaphors
* design canvases.

Tools like:

* Maslow's hierarchy
* the Lean Canvas
* systems diagrams
* abductive frameworks

act as **cognitive mediators** that help construct explanations.

In practice, I help you:

* connect ideas across domains
* use frameworks as thinking scaffolds
* transform abstract ideas into conceptual models.

---

## 4. Manipulative Abduction

**Reframing the problem itself**

This is the most radical form of abductive thinking.

Instead of solving the problem given, we **redefine the problem**.

Manipulative abduction involves:

* new assumptions
* new problem definitions
* new solution spaces.

This is where innovation often happens.

Here I help you:

* challenge the hidden assumptions in your argument
* flip the frame of the discussion
* discover more powerful questions than answers.

---

## 5. Innovative Abduction

**Creating novelty from constraints**

This form begins with **criteria or conditions** and asks:

> What new thing could satisfy these constraints?

It is often used in:

* product innovation
* design
* entrepreneurship.

Rather than explaining existing data, innovative abduction **creates something that did not previously exist**.

In our collaboration, this may appear as:

* new frameworks
* conceptual tools
* workshop exercises
* practical applications emerging from your ideas.

---

## 6. Explanatory Abduction

**Constructing models when the system is not fully known**

When reality is complex or incomplete, we build **theoretical models** that help explain how things might work.

These models enable **"preduction"** — reasoning forward from a hypothetical explanation.

Examples include:

* tectonic plate theory
* counterfactual reasoning
* conceptual models of innovation.

In this mode, I help you:

* articulate explanatory models
* build conceptual scaffolding for your arguments
* test how ideas behave under hypothetical conditions.

---

## 7. Sophisticated Abduction

**Meta-reasoning and model building**

This is the most advanced form of abductive thinking.

It operates at a **meta-level**, reasoning about reasoning itself.

It includes:

* overhypothesis formation
* counterfactual reasoning
* model ecosystems
* epistemic reflection.

Here we ask:

* What kind of explanation are we constructing?
* What assumptions shape our reasoning?
* What alternative models could exist?

This is where deep intellectual breakthroughs occur.
