# Curtis Michelson: Innovation Turning Point & SwarmVision Origin

**Origin:** Interview, April 2026
**Author:** Curtis Michelson (via structured interview)
**Tags:** backstory, innovation, SwarmVision, ExO, infoDJ, JOTF, origin-story

---

## The Pivot: Innov8rs Miami, February 2019

Curtis had been doing facilitation work for years, but the shift to *innovation* came at a specific event: the Innov8rs conference in Miami, February 2019. Being local, he called the organizers and offered to host a tour bus around Miami — wearing his facilitation hat.

But at the conference, he met kindred spirits working in corporate innovation. The most consequential connection was **Suzan Briganti**, who had just launched her company **SwarmVision**.

## The Assessment Tool That Died

A couple of years prior, Curtis had independently developed an assessment tool for business teams to evaluate their capability for strategic thinking. When he saw SwarmVision, he recognized it was the same instinct — but Suzan's tool was far more robust, scientifically validated, and enterprise-tested. It could determine not just *if* a company had innovators, but *what type* of innovators they were.

Curtis's own tool never saw the light of day again.

## SwarmVision: Innovation Typology

Suzan's framework focused on the types of innovation companies require at different stages — sometimes incremental, sometimes adjacent, sometimes disruptive. The language and rigor gave Curtis a vocabulary he'd been missing.

She invited him to join the **founding network** of SwarmVision.

## Lead Trainer & Pattern Multiplier

Curtis became SwarmVision's **lead trainer** — and trained the other trainers in the program. The methodology was:

1. **Pre-assessment** — baseline the organization's innovation profile
2. **Training program** — modular curriculum targeting specific innovation capabilities
3. **Post-assessment** — measure improvement

### The US Navy Engagement

Working with the US Navy, the pre-assessment showed they were very high on skills called **Control** and **Think** — good for near-term, incremental innovation — but rarely produced disruptive innovations (which carry the biggest and most important ROI).

After the training program, measurable improvement appeared in the quality of their ambitions, the articulation of their pitches, and their overall innovation output.

Curtis also worked with **NASA** through the SwarmVision network.

## The Pinnacle Moment: InnoLeader Impact Conference, New York

The defining moment came when Suzan brought Curtis to New York for the **Impact** conference, hosted by **InnoLeader** (Innovation Leader). Together, they delivered a one-hour workshop introducing the SwarmVision system.

In the room: heads of innovation from companies like **Chick-fil-A** and peers at that level.

> "That's where I really felt like I had landed. This is where I wanted to be."

## Parallel Thread: ExO Certification (2019)

In the same pivotal year, Curtis got certified as an **ExO (Exponential Organizations) coach** — connected to the book *Exponential Organizations*. This opened another lane: leading companies through **12-week sprints** of intensive strategic ideation and business initiative shaping.

The key discovery: there's a vital role for an external force — a disruptive thought provoker. In the ExO movement, this role is literally called **"the Disruptor."**

## The Lineage: Disruptor → infoDJ → JOTF

- **Disruptor** (ExO role) — Curtis recognized this as his natural function
- **infoDJ** (2020–2023) — Curtis's first company built around the "disruptor on demand" concept. Could not scale. Died when ChatGPT arrived.
- **JOTF / Jedi on the Fly** (2024–present) — A resuscitation of the original infoDJ vision, but now scalable through AI. Something that can work across tens, hundreds, or thousands of people in a company.

## Two 2019 Threads, One Trajectory

SwarmVision gave Curtis the **innovation assessment** lens — understanding what types of innovators exist and how to develop them. ExO gave him the **innovation culture/capability** lens — understanding the role of external disruption in organizational transformation.

Both threads converged into JOTF: AI-augmented tools that can play the disruptor role at scale, helping organizations surface signals, collide perspectives, and build innovation capability — without needing Curtis in every room.
