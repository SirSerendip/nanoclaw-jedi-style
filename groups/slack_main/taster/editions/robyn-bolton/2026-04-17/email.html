The Operating Model Is the Innovation
COLLISION: AI AS OPERATING MODEL SHIFT × AGENTIC AI DEPLOYMENT · 2 SIGNALS

Microsoft dropped a phrase this week that deserves to be tattooed on every innovation leader's whiteboard: "AI is an operating model shift, not a technology upgrade." They're arguing that AI transformation requires rethinking how work gets done at scale — with trust and people at the center. Not tools. Not pilots. The whole system. Read more →

Now layer this on top: the agentic AI market is projected to hit $45 billion by 2030, and 74% of companies plan to deploy autonomous AI agents within two years. Read more →

This is Clayton Christensen's disruption paradox playing out in real time. The sustaining innovation (better AI tools) is straightforward. The disruptive shift (autonomous agents making decisions without human gatekeepers) is where the corporate immune system will attack hardest. Because agentic AI doesn't just change processes — it threatens hierarchies. And hierarchies are what antibodies defend.

Same story in energy: Sungrow's storage systems revenue just surpassed inverter revenue for the first time — a 128% year-over-year explosion. Energy storage isn't disrupting power generation. It's enabling entirely new business models. Read more →

Rita McGrath calls these inflection points — moments when the assumptions undergirding a business shift fundamentally. For defense and energy alike, the inflection isn't the technology. The evidence is showing up in unexpected places. AI-powered drug discovery is delivering Phase 1 success rates of 80-90% compared to the historical 40-65%. Read more →

But there's a dark mirror: the IMF warns that trade fragmentation could reduce global GDP by up to 7% over a decade. When tariffs hit six-fold increases, innovation diffusion slows. The geography of who gets to innovate is being redrawn. Read more →

Clayton Christensen gave us the disruption lens. Roger Martin would push further: the strategic choice isn't disruption vs. sustaining anymore. It's disruption vs. creation vs. constraint. And tariffs are a reminder that the playing field itself is a variable.
