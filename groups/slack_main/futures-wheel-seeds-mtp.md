## Seed 1: Agentic AI envisions, designs and ships products autonomously

**Supporting Signals:**

- **Anthropic: Long-running Claude for scientific computing** (Mar 23, 2026) — Claude autonomously built a differentiable cosmological Boltzmann solver in JAX over multiple days — a working scientific software product, in a domain outside its creators' expertise, with minimal human oversight. This isn't assisted coding. It's an agent that scoped, designed, iterated, debugged, and shipped.

- **Sierra AI: Ghostwriter launch — "Agents as a service"** (Mar 25, 2026) — Sierra's Ghostwriter creates production-ready customer support agents from SOPs and call transcripts. An AI designing and shipping other AI products. The meta-layer: the product manager is the machine.

- **Wired: "Cursor launches new AI agent to take on Claude Code and Codex"** (Apr 2, 2026) — The agentic coding race is now a three-way sprint — Cursor, Claude Code, Codex — all competing on how much of the build-test-ship loop the agent can own autonomously. The PM question: when the agent ships the feature, who owns the roadmap?

## Seed 2: Machines now learn faster from experience than the people who built them

**Supporting Signals:**

- **Anthropic Research: Long-running Claude** (Mar 23, 2026) — Same signal, different angle: Claude iterated and improved code in cosmology — a domain its builders had zero expertise in. The machine ramped up in a field faster than the humans who made it could have. Learning-by-doing, not learning-by-training.

- **Microsoft Research: AsgardBench** (Mar 26, 2026) — A benchmark specifically measuring whether AI agents can adapt plans based on visual feedback in real time — testing whether agents learn from observed experience and revise when reality contradicts expectations. The benchmarking of machine learning-from-experience is now itself a product category.

- **Hugging Face / Snowflake: Ulysses Sequence Parallelism** (Mar 9, 2026) — Enables LLM training on million-token sequences by distributing attention across GPUs. Orders of magnitude more experience ingested per training pass. The speedometer on machine learning just got a new digit.

## Seed 6: Authorship is now unverifiable and everyone knows it

**Supporting Signals:**

- **Campaigns & Elections: "AI and user-generated content may decide the next majority"** (Mar 16, 2026) — Deepfakes and AI-generated political content already shaping 2026 midterms. Platforms deploying AI detection tools because authorship of political content is no longer verifiable by default. Campaigns now assume any content could be synthetic.

- **Music Business Worldwide: Deezer launches AI music detection licensing** (Mar 23, 2026) — Deezer commercially licensing an AI music detection tool specifically because AI-generated music is indistinguishable from human-created at scale. The business model: selling the ability to tell the difference. When detection is a product, verification is dead.

- **MarTech: "How Gemini in Gmail changes inbox visibility"** (Mar 3, 2026) — Gemini drafts replies, generates summaries, creates email content indistinguishable from human-written. For the PM audience: your users' customers can no longer tell who wrote the message. Authorship in business communication is now a UX question, not a fact.

**Supporting Signals:**

- **CFO Dive: "Nearly 1 in 3 finance roles now require AI skills"** (Mar 24, 2026) — One-third of finance job postings require skills that exist in no traditional finance credential. The report explicitly states AI is "redefining finance functions." Your CPA doesn't prove you can do the job.

- **Government CIO: Tech Force — skills-based federal hiring** (Mar 19, 2026) — OPM's Tech Force abandons credential-based hiring for skills-based assessments. - **Nextgov: IRS faces AI skills gaps after losing 40% of IT staff** (Mar 24, 2026) — The IRS lost 40% of IT staff and 80% of executives — all presumably credentialed. Now cannot execute its AI strategy. The credentials didn't map to the capability needed. The remaining credentialed workforce can't fill the void.

## Seed 8: For the first time, not knowing where to start is the only real barrier

**Supporting Signals:**

- **Ad Exchanger: "AI perfectionism is slowing marketing down — decision velocity is the new advantage"** (Mar 25, 2026) — Access to AI tools is no longer the bottleneck. The barrier is now decision paralysis — too many options, not knowing which AI approach to adopt first. Teams delay action waiting for perfect. The barrier shifted from capability to orientation. This is THE PM signal.

- **New America / Route Fifty: "AI risks creating a demand machine for governments"** (Mar 3, 2026) — AI lowered barriers to building and deploying services to near zero. Result: latent demand surfaces faster than organizations can prioritize. The barrier isn't technical capability anymore — it's knowing where to direct unlimited capacity.

- **Government CIO: "Federal AI Series — Training and the Workforce"** (Mar 12, 2026) — Zscaler's CTO: traditional training has failed for AI adoption — not because tools are inaccessible, but because people don't know how to begin applying AI to their specific mission. The proposed solution (prompt-athons, hands-on use cases) explicitly addresses the "where to start" problem.
