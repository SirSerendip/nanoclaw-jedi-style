### About the Value Discovery Game

I realize that in the excitement of the demo, I may not have described the game as clearly as you deserved. So let me give you the version you'd give a client:

**The Value Discovery Game** is a facilitated strategic exercise that helps leadership teams see the hidden value flows in their business ecosystem — the relationships, exchanges, and dependencies that don't show up on an org chart or a balance sheet.

Here's how it works:
- **Round 1 — Mapping:** Teams identify their key stakeholders and map the tangible exchanges between them (money, products, services). This is the visible economy.
- **Round 2 — Discovery:** This is where it gets interesting. Teams surface the *intangible* value flows — trust, knowledge, referrals, reputation, emotional loyalty. These are often the real differentiators, but they're invisible until you name them.
- **Round 3 — AI Coaching:** Three AI coaches offer different lenses on the map:
  - **Coach Verna** (inspired by Verna Allee's Value Network Analysis) examines the structural health of the network
  - **Coach Anna** (inspired by your Demand Perspective) challenges teams to see from the outside in — what does the customer actually experience? Where does emotional connection create business value?
  - **Coach Jamais** (inspired by Jamais Cascio's BANI framework) stress-tests the network against future disruption

The game typically runs in 60-90 minutes with 4-8 participants and produces a visual network map with AI-generated strategic insights. Teams walk away with a concrete artifact — not just a conversation, but a map they can act on.

**Who it's for:** Mid-market companies and growth-stage organizations going through transition — new markets, changing customer expectations, post-acquisition integration, digital transformation. Exactly the "smelling smoke" companies you and I both know so well.

**What makes it different:** Most strategy tools are either too abstract (frameworks without data) or too rigid (software without facilitation). The Value Discovery Game sits in the middle — it's structured enough to produce results and flexible enough to surface what's actually in the room. And the AI coaches don't replace the human facilitator — they add perspectives that no single facilitator could bring alone.
